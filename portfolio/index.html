Experiences - Planning and Design

Our Lady of Fatima Chapel is located in Osiem Mangaldan, Pangasinan. The existing chapel was built around 1986. For over 3 decades, multiple damages such as huge cracks on the walls, columns and floors. There's also no roof insulation on existing chapel that results to increasing heat and temperature within the chapel. These gives stress to people going inside the chapel.

The congregation views chapels as peacefull, relaxing and a stress free environment. They wanted people to viwe chapel as their second home and believed that with this kind of environment more people would be engaged to gather and listen to the words of God.

Oak White
Washed exterior is one of the main concept of the chapel to retain some aspectes of the old chapel. Oak white wash also uplift the ambiance upon entering the chapel.

Also the only hue that does not attract heat is white. Because white reflects all the visible wavelengths to light. Therefore it is one of the best solution to reduce heat within the building

Natural
Reconnecting people to nature through architecture. As more people crave connection to the natural world we tend to divide it more with buildings. Thus the design reflects more of returning to nature

Natural designs are comprise of materials using more timber/wood finishings, this has been more specifically applied at the interiors of the chapel. Brick is also applied at the facade of the chapel.

The Scheme
Shows the combined concepts and research. The result shown is more a-frame type of roof and adding a truss that extends and bolted at the pedestal.

A-frame type of roof is good for better circulation of air and lowers buildings temperature as heat air rises. It also lessen moisture esp on hot humid day. The roof is also provided with fiber glass skylight to release the heat accumulated within the building.

The Schematic Plan
The illustrated rough schematic plan shows the basic scale, form, sizes, and areas of the chapel. Also provided the actual sizes of the existing chapel.

Provided areas of the chapel are the worship area, counseling area, staff support area, public areas, and office
